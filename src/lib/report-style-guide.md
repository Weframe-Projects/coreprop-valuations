# CoreProp Report Style Guide

Extracted from 5 real valuation certificates. Use this to tune AI prompts.

## Reports analyzed
1. **2B Colville Terrace, W11 2BE** — Auction val, 1-bed first floor flat, period conversion, London (leasehold, new 125yr)
2. **Elm Park, Pontefract, WF8 4LG** — IHT val, detached bungalow, Yorkshire (freehold)
3. **89 Rosebank Avenue, Hornchurch, RM12 5** — Current + Retrospective val, 3-bed semi, Essex (freehold)
4. **115 Percy Avenue, Broadstairs, CT10 3LD** — Current market val, detached care home/house, Kent (freehold)
5. **Victoria Road, NW6 6** — Auction val, 1-bed first floor flat, period conversion, NW London (leasehold, new 125yr)

---

## Section 5 — Description of Property

### Pattern (inspected)
- 5.1: Property type + position + location character (1 sentence)
- 5.2+: Additional context paragraphs — street description, area character, transport links, planning notes, improvement works
- Varies significantly between reports — simple properties get 1 paragraph, complex ones (care home, bungalow with extensions) get 4-5 paragraphs

### Key phrases
- "self-contained [floor] floor flat, positioned within a [position] terrace period conversion"
- "located in a populated [and desirable] [area] location, close to local amenities"
- "detached bungalow, located in a tucked away position"
- "[Street] is a desirable residential street located in [area] within [borough]"

---

## Section 6 — Construction

### Pattern (always 3 sub-sections)
- 6.1: Storeys, construction type, external finish, architectural features, roof type
- 6.2: Sub-flooring type ("predominantly of timber/solid construction")
- 6.3: ALWAYS ends with "The method of construction is traditional for the type and age of the Property."

### Key phrases
- "The Building is arranged over [N] storeys"
- "of traditional masonry construction"
- "The principal elevation is finished in [material]"
- "surmounted by a pitched [material] roof"
- "as identified from external inspection and [satellite imagery / aerial imagery]"

---

## Section 7 — Accommodation

### Pattern (always same)
- Opening: "Accommodation within the Property comprises as follows:"
- Then floor-by-floor list: "Ground Floor: Hallway, Kitchen, Living / Dining Room"
- One line per floor, rooms comma-separated

---

## Section 8 — Externally

### Pattern (2-3 sub-sections)
- 8.1: Front — approach, path, entrance
- 8.2: Parking — off-street, permit, garage
- 8.3: Rear — garden description, features
- For flats: simpler (communal entrance, no demised parking, permit holders only)

### Key phrases
- "To the front of the Building/Property is..."
- "providing off street parking for c.[N] vehicle(s)"
- "There are parking bays directly outside the Building for 'Permit Holders Only'"
- "To the rear of the Property is an enclosed garden, comprising..."

---

## Section 13 — Condition & Further Details

### Pattern (ALWAYS these sub-sections in this order)
- 13.1: Opening: "In preparing [the/our] Valuation Report, we have [had] regard for the following matters [as at the date of inspection]:"
- 13.2: Kitchen — "[condition] with [fittings detail]"
- 13.3: Bathroom — "[condition] and in a [quality] condition"
- 13.4: Heating — "The [internal] heating mechanisms and gas [fired] boiler ([brand] combination) are in [condition] (not tested albeit assumed functional)"
- 13.5: Flooring — "The flooring [is laid with / within the Property is laid with] a mix[ture] of [materials]"
- 13.6: Electrical — "The electrical wiring installation [in the Property] [appears to comply with / requires upgrading in order to comply with] current NICEIC [safety specification] standards (not tested)"
- 13.7: Windows — "The window installations are [material] framed [glazing] units in a [condition]"
- 13.8: Decorative — "The [internal] decorative condition of the Property is [considered] [condition]"
- 13.9: General issues (if any) — defects, water ingress, specialist investigation
- 13.10: Overall — "The [general/overall] condition of the Property is considered [condition]. [modernisation statement if needed]"

### Condition vocabulary
- poor / basic / dated / serviceable / fair / good / modern / mid spec
- "in need of modernisation"
- "If placed onto the open market, it is expected that any incoming purchaser would immediately update the interior"
- "well maintained" / "generally well maintained internally"

---

## Section 14 — Structure and External

### Pattern (always 2-3 sub-sections)
- 14.1: External elevations — construction type, condition, weathering, structural movement assessment
- 14.2: Painted/decorative external elements — wear, cyclical maintenance recommendation
- 14.3: Roof — condition, age estimate, "not intrusively inspected" disclaimer

### Key phrases
- "exhibiting light weathering and age-related discolouration consistent with a property of this type and age"
- "No obvious signs of significant structural movement were apparent"
- "routine cyclical maintenance and periodic redecoration should be anticipated"
- "The roof appears to be in a reasonable condition for its [original] age, c.30+ years visibly weathered (not intrusively inspected and viewed only from [street level / google satellite view])"

---

## Section 15 — Amenity

### Pattern (always same structure)
- 15.1: Area character: "The Property is in a [populated/established] [residential/coastal] area, with neighbo[u]ring dwellings of [compatible/mixed] style and size."
- 15.2: Transport: "Principal transport facilities close by include:" then list with distances
- 15.3: Amenities: "Schools, [parks/beaches] and other amenities close by."

### Distance format
- Station names with distance and mode: "[Station] c.[X.X] miles distant (walking)."
- Hospital if relevant: "[Hospital] c.[X.X] miles distant ([walking/car])."

---

## Section 17 — Valuation Conclusions & Market Commentary

### Pattern
- Market commentary paragraphs (7-8 paragraphs covering inflation, rates, housing market, commercial, cost of living, policy)
- NOTE: These are saved in Settings and are the SAME across all reports for the same period
- IHT reports use period-specific commentary (Q3/Q4 2025 vs Q1 2026)
- Material valuation uncertainty paragraph (always included)
- Appendix 1 reference
- Final valuation conclusion with figure in words

### Auction reports additionally include
- Reserve price paragraph with explanation of strategy
- "If the Property is to be sold via a public auction, we recommend an auction reserve in [months] at the sum of £[X]"

### Multi-valuation reports (retrospective + current)
- Two separate conclusion paragraphs with different dates and figures

---

## Photo placement (from PDF analysis)
- Front elevation photo: after Section 5-6 (Description + Construction)
- Exterior/garden photos: after Section 7-8 (Accommodation + Externally)
- Kitchen + Bathroom + Condition photos: after Section 13 (Condition)
- Comparable images: within Section 16 (if available)
- Remaining photos: Photo appendix after Section 15

---

## Notes for images mentioned in reports
- Reports reference "Image overleaf" next to comparable entries when images are included
- Comparable photos appear to be inserted after the comparable data table
- Google Street View used as fallback for front elevation photo
- Google satellite imagery referenced for roof condition assessment
